Touching your beautiful home with hardwood floors is a brilliant idea that increases the value of your property. It is an investment that you want to pretect. You will want to keep the value at the peak condition while enjoy the comfortness it brings over the years.

The key to maintain the healthy condition of the hardwood floors is the environment. Keeping the room at a stable environment, with the room temperature between 60° to 80° F and humidity range of 30-50% is recommended year round.

Pets

Dog's or cat's nails are very sharp. They can scratch any finish. So keep the nails clipped.

Also, standing water or liquid is a hazard to wood. A mat under your pet's water dish is a good idea. And don't forget to clean up pets' "nature waste" as soon as possible, or train them well so they don't do it in house.

Area Rug Backings

Rubber or abrasive backing is bad for hardwood floors because they could damage or discolor the floors. Use rugs with a natural backing

During the winter months of low humidity, minor surface cracks may appear in wood flooring, then often close up again in the summer months when the humidity is higher.

This is a normal for natural wood. If there is no structural failure, you don't have to complain to the manufacturer.

Lacquering and Waxing/Oiling are different ways of finishing the wood floor. Comparing to the old fashioned waxed/oiled finishes, which require regular treatment to keep them maintained, the modern lacquered wood floor has more attractive features, such as:

  • Tough and hardwearing surface
  • Easy to clean
  • 0 or minimum effort to maintain
  • Preserved nature color

Hardwood floor products in nowadays are easy to maintain. Besides the environmental control, regular cleaning is all it needs. Simple sweeping, dust mopping, and vacuuming will keep your floor looks like new for years.

CAUTION: It is important to only use a cleaning product compatible with the type of finish of your flooring.

Your purchase usually comes with maitenance instructions. Carefully follow the these instructions. Avoid using a wet mop as over time this can damage the finish.

All purpose cleaners are also not recommended as they can dull your floor's finish or leave a hazy residue.

What is good for the hardwood floor:

  • Regular sweeping, dust mopping or vacuuming (disengage the vacuum cleaner's beater bar, which is the rotating bristle brush, before use it on the hardwood floor).
  • Removing liquid spills immediately with cloth or paper towels.
  • An area rug in front of the kitchen sink, to catch water.
  • Maintaining with the appropriate floor cleaner.
  • Limited direct sun exposure.
  • Maintaining room temperature (60°­–80° F).
  • Maintaining relative humidity between 30-50%.
  • Felt protectors or furniture coasters under heavy furniture.
  • Caster wheeled chairs should have wide casters.
  • A protective mat under office chairs.
  • In areas with icy or snowy winters, extra protection against salt & grit may be needed.
  • Mats and throw rugs at doorways, exteriors and interiors. This helps preventing the tracking of grit, dirt and sand.

What is bad for the hardwood floor:

  • Oil based soaps.
  • Paste wax based products (NEVER wax a lacquered finished floor).
  • Steam cleaners. They are not recommended for use on natural wood flooring.
  • Wet mopping. Standing water can dull a finish, damage the wood and leave a discoloring residue.
  • Dragging sharp wooden legs or metal furniture legs, as it can scratch/dent hardwood floors.
  • Moving heavy furniture without protecting wood flooring by slipping a piece of cloth or pile under the legs or bottom of items.
  • Cleats, sport shoes and high heels. They can dent any floor surface.
  • Placing porous flower pots or vases on the floor.
  • Steel wool or scourers.

When hardwood floor ages, its color may change from its original color. This may caused by exposure to the sunlight, heat, or air conditioning. This is the nature of hardwood floor. It is not defect.

When an area is covered with large furnitures or rugs, the covered hardwood floor may have less color changing than the area around it. If possible, rotate the locations of the furnitures or rugs regularly to avoid this unwanted visual impact.

Seasonal gapping can be expected, especially on wider planks. This is normal and not a defect.

Environmental conditions is the main reason why the seasonal gapping can happen. The wood will expand or contract to the changes of temperature and/or humidity. Keeping a comparatively stable temperature and relative humidity is important to minimum these gappings.
